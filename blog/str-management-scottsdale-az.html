The Scottsdale Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Owners

Scottsdale consistently ranks among the top short-term rental markets in the United States, fueled by a diverse mix of leisure travelers, corporate visitors, and event-goers. The city attracts guests for Cactus League spring training, the Barrett-Jackson auto auction, major golf tournaments, and a vibrant Old Town nightlife and dining scene — creating multiple high-demand windows throughout the year.

Popular neighborhoods for STR investors include Old Town Scottsdale, McCormick Ranch, Gainey Ranch, and the areas surrounding TPC Scottsdale. Properties in these corridors typically benefit from strong occupancy and above-average nightly rates. Many operators in markets like Scottsdale report average daily rates (ADR) ranging from $200 to $400 or more for well-appointed homes, with occupancy rates frequently exceeding 65–75% on an annual basis.

What are the regulations for short-term rentals in Scottsdale, AZ?

Scottsdale requires short-term rental operators to obtain a transaction privilege tax (TPT) license and comply with Arizona state STR laws, which generally prohibit cities from banning STRs outright but allow for noise, safety, and neighbor-impact regulations. Property owners must also collect and remit applicable local and state taxes on rental income.
