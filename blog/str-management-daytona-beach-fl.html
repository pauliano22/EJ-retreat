The Daytona Beach Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Daytona Beach sits at a rare intersection of event-driven demand and steady leisure travel. The Daytona International Speedway alone generates multiple high-demand weekends annually, including the Daytona 500 and the Coke Zero Sugar 400, when nightly rates across the market surge significantly. Beyond racing, Biketoberfest in October and Bike Week in March reliably fill beachside properties, giving owners multiple revenue spikes outside of summer.

Popular neighborhoods for short-term rentals include the Beachside corridor along Atlantic Avenue, the Daytona Beach Shores strip south of the main tourist zone, and the area surrounding the Speedway for event visitors who prioritize proximity over ocean views. Markets like Daytona Beach often see occupancy rates ranging from 55% to 75% annually for well-positioned listings, with average daily rates that climb well above baseline during signature events.

  • Year-round demand anchored by motorsports, spring break, and beach tourism
  • Multiple high-ADR event weekends that reward dynamic pricing strategies
  • Diverse inventory from oceanfront condos to single-family homes near the Speedway
  • Growing traveler preference for STRs over hotels in leisure-heavy destinations

Revenue Potential for Short-Term Rentals in Daytona Beach

Revenue in the Daytona Beach short-term rental market varies based on property type, location, and how well the listing is managed, but the upside is substantial for owners who optimize correctly. Beachfront and ocean-view condos typically command the highest average daily rates, with many operators reporting nightly rates that double or triple during major Speedway events and holiday weekends. Seasonal demand in Daytona Beach follows a predictable pattern — summer and event weekends drive peak revenue, while shoulder months like November and January tend to see softer occupancy. Professional management bridges the gap by using targeted promotions, flexible minimum-night strategies, and last-minute pricing to fill calendar gaps that self-managing owners often leave empty.
