Houston's Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Investors

Houston consistently ranks among the top U.S. cities for short-term rental demand, driven by a uniquely diverse mix of travelers. The Texas Medical Center — the largest medical complex in the world — brings a steady flow of patients, families, and healthcare professionals who prefer furnished STRs over hotels for extended stays. Corporate relocations tied to the energy industry, conventions at the George R. Brown Convention Center, and major sports events at venues like Minute Maid Park and NRG Stadium layer additional demand on top of that baseline.

Popular Houston neighborhoods for STRs include Midtown, Montrose, the Heights, and the Museum District, where walkability, dining, and nightlife attract leisure travelers. Meanwhile, properties near the Medical Center and Greenway Plaza draw longer-stay business guests. Many operators in markets like Houston report average daily rates (ADRs) ranging from $120 to $220 depending on property size, location, and amenities, with well-managed listings often achieving occupancy rates above 70 percent. Key demand drivers include:

  • Year-round medical and corporate travel
  • Major conventions, rodeos, and sporting events
  • A growing arts and culinary tourism scene
  • Strong inbound relocation activity from out-of-state buyers

Revenue Potential for Short-Term Rentals in Houston, TX

Revenue potential in Houston varies by neighborhood, property size, and how well the listing is managed, but the fundamentals are favorable. A professionally managed one-bedroom in Midtown or Montrose can typically generate gross revenue in the range of $2,000 to $3,500 per month, while larger three- or four-bedroom homes near the Medical Center or in the Heights often exceed $5,000 per month during strong periods. Many Houston operators report that professional management unlocks 20 to 35 percent more revenue than self-managing, largely because of optimized pricing and multi-channel distribution.

Houston's demand calendar creates natural peaks that skilled managers exploit. The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo in late winter, major medical conferences, and fall football season consistently push occupancy and rates higher. Summer months draw family leisure travel and relocation activity. By contrast, the weeks immediately following major holidays can soften — a dynamic pricing strategy that adjusts floors and minimums during those periods helps protect overall monthly revenue.

Compared to self-managing, working with E&J Retreats means your Houston property is always priced at or near its market ceiling, never sitting vacant due to calendar gaps, and presented with professional-quality content that earns the bookings competing listings miss.

Do I need a permit or license to operate a short-term rental in Houston?

Houston currently does not require a city-issued STR permit the way some other major Texas cities do, but property owners must still comply with applicable HOA rules, deed restrictions, and collect and remit hotel occupancy taxes to both the city and the state.
