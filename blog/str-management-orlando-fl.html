Orlando's Short-Term Rental Market: Why Investors Keep Coming Back

Few markets in the United States match Orlando for sheer, sustained short-term rental demand. The region hosts Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld, the Orange County Convention Center, and a growing medical and tech corridor — meaning demand spans leisure travelers, convention attendees, and business visitors throughout the year.

Popular Orlando STR neighborhoods and micro-markets include Kissimmee, Champions Gate, Reunion Resort, Davenport, Lake Buena Vista, and the Dr. Phillips area near Restaurant Row. Properties in these zones typically benefit from proximity to the theme park corridor, which drives strong repeat bookings and longer average stays.

  • Markets like Orlando often see occupancy rates of 65–80% for well-positioned properties
  • Average daily rates (ADR) typically range from $120 to $300+ depending on home size and amenities
  • Year-round demand reduces the seasonal income volatility common in other STR markets
  • Strong guest review cultures in Orlando mean quality management directly impacts ranking and revenue

Revenue Potential for Orlando Short-Term Rentals

Orlando's tourism engine creates revenue opportunities that many STR markets simply cannot match. Many operators report that well-managed three- and four-bedroom homes near the theme park corridor generate gross annual revenues in the range of $40,000 to $90,000 or more, depending on property size, amenities, and proximity to key attractions.

Seasonal demand in Orlando peaks around major school holidays — summer, spring break, Thanksgiving, and Christmas — but the market's convention calendar and international visitor base help keep occupancy elevated even in slower months. Properties with private pools, game rooms, or resort community access typically command higher ADR and attract longer bookings.

Do I need a license or permit to run a short-term rental in Orlando?

Yes — short-term rental operators in Orlando and the surrounding Orange and Osceola County areas are typically required to obtain a state vacation rental license through the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR), as well as applicable local business tax receipts. Requirements can vary by municipality, so it is important to confirm the rules for your specific address before listing. E&J Retreats can help guide new owners through the compliance process.
