Fort Lauderdale's Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Owners

Fort Lauderdale draws a remarkably diverse traveler mix — from spring breakers and cruise passengers departing Port Everglades to yacht enthusiasts, corporate travelers, and snowbirds escaping northern winters. This diversity of demand helps smooth out seasonal dips that affect more narrowly focused beach markets, keeping occupancy elevated for much of the year.

Popular neighborhoods for short-term rentals in Fort Lauderdale include Las Olas Isles, Victoria Park, Lauderdale-by-the-Sea, and the barrier island stretching along A1A. Properties near the Intracoastal Waterway or with private docks command a premium, while homes and condos within walking distance of the beach or Las Olas Boulevard attract strong leisure bookings. Markets like Fort Lauderdale often see average daily rates well above the national STR average, particularly during winter and spring.

  • Year-round demand from beach tourism, cruise travelers, and corporate visitors
  • Strong seasonal peaks in winter (snowbirds), spring (spring break), and major boat show season
  • Waterfront and beach-adjacent properties typically achieve the highest ADR
  • Growing population and infrastructure investment continue to support STR demand

Yes — Fort Lauderdale and the state of Florida both have registration and licensing requirements for short-term rental operators, including a Florida Department of Revenue sales tax account and a Broward County Tourist Development Tax registration.
