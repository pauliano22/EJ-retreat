Boynton Beach's Short-Term Rental Market: A Growing Coastal Opportunity

Palm Beach County's STR market has grown steadily as domestic travel demand in South Florida has expanded. Boynton Beach benefits from Atlantic beachfront access at Oceanfront Park, the Boynton Beach Inlet for boating and fishing, and convenient access to I-95 and the Florida Turnpike — making it an easy base for guests exploring the Gold Coast.

The city draws a diverse mix of travelers: winter "snowbirds" seeking week-long or monthly escapes from the Northeast and Midwest, families wanting a more affordable alternative to Miami's premium rates, and domestic vacationers drawn to Palm Beach County's dining, nightlife, and outdoor recreation. Properties near the Intracoastal Waterway or within walking distance of the beach command premium nightly rates, especially during the January–April high season.

What are Boynton Beach's short-term rental regulations?

Short-term rentals in Boynton Beach are regulated at both the city and Palm Beach County level. Owners typically need a local business tax receipt and must comply with Florida's state vacation rental statutes, which require registration with the Department of Business and Professional Regulation. Regulations evolve frequently, and non-compliance can result in fines or platform deactivation.
