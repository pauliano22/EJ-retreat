The Telluride Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Telluride's STR market benefits from a rare combination of strong seasonal peaks and a growing shoulder-season appeal. Winter brings world-class skiing at Telluride Ski Resort, while summer attracts attendees of the Telluride Film Festival, Bluegrass Festival, and a packed calendar of outdoor events. This multi-season draw gives well-positioned properties occupancy patterns that many single-season mountain markets simply cannot match.

Popular areas for short-term rentals include Mountain Village, the historic downtown core, and gondola-accessible neighborhoods that command premium nightly rates. Many operators in markets like Telluride report average daily rates (ADR) well above national STR averages, given the destination's luxury positioning and limited lodging supply.

  • Multi-season demand driven by skiing, festivals, and hiking
  • Limited new hotel supply keeps vacation rental demand high
  • Proximity to gondola and ski lifts significantly boosts nightly rates
  • Affluent traveler base willing to pay for quality accommodations

Frequently Asked Questions: STR Management in Telluride, Colorado

How much can I earn from a short-term rental in Telluride, Colorado?

Earnings vary by property size, location, and management quality, but Telluride is one of Colorado's highest-earning STR markets. Do I need a license or permit to operate a short-term rental in Telluride?

Yes — the Town of Telluride and San Miguel County each have STR licensing requirements, and rules can differ depending on whether your property is within town limits or in the surrounding county. E&J Retreats helps owners understand local compliance obligations, though we recommend confirming current requirements directly with the relevant municipal office before listing. Staying current on regulations protects your investment and avoids costly fines.
