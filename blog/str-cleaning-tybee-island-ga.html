STR Turnover Cleaning on Tybee Island: Unique Challenges and Rising Standards

Tybee Island's coastal environment creates cleaning demands that go well beyond a standard residential tidy. Sand tracks through every room, salt air accelerates mildew on linens and grout, and guests arriving from the beach expect spotless bathrooms and fresh bedding — not evidence of the party that checked out four hours ago. Many operators in markets like Tybee Island report that guest complaints about cleanliness are the single most common driver of low reviews.

STR turnover cleaning is a distinct discipline from routine housekeeping. Every clean must meet a consistent, documented standard regardless of how much time the previous guests left behind or how tight the same-day window is. That means:

  • Full linen strip and replacement — not just a refresh
  • Deep bathroom and kitchen sanitization between every stay
  • Sand and debris removal from floors, outdoor showers, and entryways
  • Restocking of guest supplies and amenities to a set par level
  • Damage and inventory checks reported to the host before the next arrival

Unreliable cleaning in a high-velocity market like Tybee Island compounds quickly. One missed turnover or inconsistent crew can produce a string of bad reviews, trigger platform penalties, and erode the nightly rate you have worked to build.
