The Kissimmee Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Kissimmee consistently ranks among the strongest short-term rental markets in the United States, fueled by its proximity to world-class theme parks and year-round warm weather. Demand is remarkably diversified — families, international tourists, sports travelers, and convention attendees all book stays in the area, smoothing out the seasonal swings that plague other vacation markets.

Popular STR corridors in Kissimmee include the resort communities along US-192, the Four Corners area near ChampionsGate, and gated vacation communities such as Windsor Hills, Reunion Resort, and Storey Lake. These neighborhoods are purpose-built for short-term rental activity, with HOA-approved STR allowances and resort-style amenities that command premium nightly rates.

Do I need a license or permit to operate a short-term rental in Kissimmee?

Yes — short-term rental operators in Kissimmee and Osceola County are generally required to obtain a state vacation rental license through the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR) as well as register for local tourist development tax collection. Specific requirements can vary depending on whether your property falls within city limits or an unincorporated area.
