The Flagstaff Short-Term Rental Market: Why Investors Are Taking Notice

Flagstaff occupies a rare position in Arizona real estate: a high-elevation mountain city that attracts demand in every season. Summer visitors escape the desert heat, fall travelers come for foliage and hiking, winter guests head to Snowbowl for skiing, and spring sees steady traffic from Grand Canyon tourists. That four-season pull means well-positioned Flagstaff STRs rarely experience the prolonged off-seasons common in single-draw destinations.

Popular neighborhoods for short-term rentals include the Southside historic district, the areas surrounding Northern Arizona University, and the wooded residential streets close to Fort Tuthill County Park. Markets like Flagstaff often see average daily rates that outperform similarly sized cities because of the destination appeal and limited hotel inventory during peak weekends.

  • Year-round demand anchored by Grand Canyon tourism and NAU events
  • Strong ski season driving premium winter rates at Arizona Snowbowl
  • Limited new hotel supply keeping STR occupancy competitive
  • Growing remote-worker and "workcation" travel segment
