The Charlotte Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Charlotte has quietly become one of the most attractive cities in the country for short-term rental investors. As the largest city in North Carolina and home to major corporate headquarters — including Bank of America and Truist Financial — the metro generates a steady stream of business travelers who prefer STRs over traditional hotels. Add in Charlotte FC soccer matches, NASCAR events at Charlotte Motor Speedway, and concerts at Spectrum Center, and event-driven demand keeps calendars full throughout the year.

Popular neighborhoods for STR investment include NoDa, South End, Dilworth, Plaza Midwood, and Uptown, where walkability and local character command premium nightly rates. Markets like Charlotte often see average daily rates ranging from the mid-$100s to over $250 for well-positioned properties, with many operators reporting occupancy rates above 65 percent annually.

Charlotte requires short-term rental operators to register with the city and comply with zoning rules that vary by property type and neighborhood. Whole-home rentals are generally permitted in residential zones, though hosts must collect and remit local occupancy taxes.
