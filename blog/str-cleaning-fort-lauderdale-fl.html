STR Turnover Cleaning Standards and Challenges in Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale's rental calendar creates a cleaning environment that is fundamentally different from routine residential housekeeping. Properties in neighborhoods like Victoria Park, Rio Vista, and the beach corridor typically turn over multiple times per week during peak season, and same-day turnovers — where a guest checks out at 10 a.m. and a new guest arrives by 4 p.m. — are common rather than exceptional. Every clean must meet hotel-level standards within a compressed window, with no margin for missed linens, overlooked bathrooms, or forgotten restocking.

The expectations guests bring to Fort Lauderdale vacation rentals are high. Markets like Fort Lauderdale often see guests comparing their stay directly to resort experiences, meaning cleanliness issues surface immediately in reviews. Many operators report that the majority of their one- and two-star reviews trace back to cleaning failures rather than property features.

  • Sand, sunscreen, and saltwater residue require targeted attention after every beach-adjacent stay
  • Outdoor spaces, pools, and boat docks add surface area that residential cleaners typically overlook
  • Linen condition and fragrance are frequently cited in guest feedback
  • Restocking consumables — soaps, paper goods, coffee — must be verified at every turnover
