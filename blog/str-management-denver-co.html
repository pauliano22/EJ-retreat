Denver's Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Owners

Denver consistently ranks among the top U.S. cities for short-term rental investment, thanks to its year-round appeal as a gateway to the Rocky Mountains and a thriving urban destination in its own right. Demand drivers include skiing and snowboarding traffic in winter, hiking and festivals in summer, and a robust convention and business travel calendar that keeps midweek occupancy healthy throughout the year.

Popular Denver neighborhoods for STR investors include Capitol Hill, RiNo (River North Art District), LoHi, the Highlands, and areas close to Coors Field and Ball Arena. Many operators in markets like Denver report average daily rates ranging from $130 to $250 or more, with occupancy rates typically in the 65–80% range for professionally managed listings.

  • Strong corporate and convention travel base

Revenue Potential for Short-Term Rentals in Denver, Colorado

Well-managed short-term rentals in Denver can generate significantly more revenue than traditional long-term leases, particularly for properties in high-demand neighborhoods or near major attractions. Many Denver STR owners report annual gross revenues ranging from $30,000 to $80,000 or more depending on property size, location, and quality of management — figures that self-managing owners often leave on the table without the right tools.

Seasonal demand patterns in Denver follow a dual-peak calendar: winter brings ski-season visitors heading to nearby resorts, while summer drives high occupancy from hikers, festival attendees, and road-trippers exploring Colorado. Spring and fall shoulder seasons are supported by Denver's strong convention calendar and growing reputation as a food, beer, and arts destination.

Professional management unlocks higher ADR and occupancy by combining smart pricing, faster review response rates, and a polished guest experience that earns five-star reviews — the single biggest driver of ranking and bookings on Airbnb and competing platforms.

Does Denver require a license to operate a short-term rental?

Yes, Denver requires hosts to obtain a Short-Term Rental license and mandates that the property be the owner's primary residence, which sets Denver apart from many other STR markets.
