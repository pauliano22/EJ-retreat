The Sedona Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Investors

Sedona attracts a diverse mix of travelers — couples on romantic getaways, wellness seekers drawn to the area's famous vortex sites, mountain bikers, hikers, and luxury tourists looking for a distinctive Southwest experience. This broad appeal helps keep demand relatively stable across multiple seasons, reducing the vacancy risk that can hurt STR investors in more single-use destinations.

Popular areas for short-term rentals in Sedona include the Village of Oak Creek, West Sedona, and the Uptown corridor, each offering different price points and guest profiles. Many operators in markets like Sedona report average daily rates (ADR) well above state and national averages, with premium properties commanding even higher rates during peak spring and fall seasons.

  • Strong year-round leisure travel demand
  • Limited hotel inventory relative to visitor volume
  • High willingness-to-pay among Sedona's largely affluent visitor base
  • Multiple demand drivers: hiking, wellness retreats, photography, romantic escapes

Revenue Potential for Well-Managed Short-Term Rentals in Sedona

Sedona's combination of high visitor spending and limited accommodation supply creates favorable conditions for STR revenue. Markets like Sedona often see occupancy rates in the 60–80% range for well-managed properties, with ADR typically ranging from $200 to $500 or more per night depending on property size, location, and amenities. Premium homes with red rock views or private pools can push significantly above those benchmarks.

Demand in Sedona follows a recognizable seasonal rhythm — spring wildflower season and fall foliage periods are peak times, while summer brings a second wave of visitors escaping lower-elevation desert heat. Professional management helps owners capitalize on these peaks through precise rate increases while maintaining healthy occupancy in shoulder months through strategic discounting and longer minimum stays.

Many self-managing owners who switch to professional STR management in Sedona report meaningful revenue increases, largely because consistent pricing strategy, faster guest turnovers, and wider platform distribution recover bookings that would otherwise be missed.

Do I need a permit or license to operate a short-term rental in Sedona, AZ?

Yes, the City of Sedona requires short-term rental operators to obtain a Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT) license from the Arizona Department of Revenue and to register the property with the city.
