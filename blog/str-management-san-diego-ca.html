The San Diego Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

San Diego consistently ranks among the top-performing short-term rental markets in the United States. The city's Mediterranean climate drives demand not just in summer but throughout the fall and spring shoulder seasons, giving investors more earning days per year than most comparable coastal markets. Neighborhoods like Pacific Beach, Mission Beach, La Jolla, and Ocean Beach attract high-intent leisure travelers willing to pay a premium for proximity to the water.

Beyond beach tourism, San Diego draws a steady stream of business travelers, military families, and attendees of major conventions at the San Diego Convention Center. This diversified demand base helps stabilize occupancy even when one traveler segment softens. Many operators in markets like San Diego report average daily rates well above the national STR average, particularly for well-appointed properties near the coast.

Do I need a permit or license to run a short-term rental in San Diego?

Yes, the City of San Diego requires short-term rental operators to obtain a Short-Term Residential Occupancy (STRO) license, and regulations vary depending on whether the property is your primary residence and how many nights per year you intend to rent. E&J Retreats strongly recommends consulting a local attorney or the City of San Diego's Development Services Department to ensure your property is compliant before listing. Staying current with local STR regulations is an important part of protecting your investment.
