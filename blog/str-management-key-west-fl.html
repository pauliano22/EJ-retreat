The Key West Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Key West benefits from a nearly year-round tourism economy, making it one of the most resilient short-term rental markets in the southeastern United States. Demand drivers include the annual Fantasy Fest celebration, Hemingway Days, the Key West Food and Wine Festival, and a steady flow of cruise ship passengers and independent travelers seeking the island's unique blend of culture and natural beauty.

Popular neighborhoods for STR investors include Old Town, with its iconic conch-style homes and walkable access to Duval Street, as well as Midtown and New Town for guests who prefer quieter surroundings. Markets like Key West often see occupancy rates well above national averages, with many operators reporting peak-season occupancy above 90 percent and average daily rates that routinely reach several hundred dollars per night.

  • Strong leisure demand from both domestic and international travelers
  • Limited hotel inventory that keeps STR occupancy elevated
  • High repeat-visitor rates driven by the island's distinctive character
  • Proximity to the Florida Keys coral reef and water-sport attractions

Revenue Potential for Short-Term Rentals in Key West

Do I need a license or permit to operate a short-term rental in Key West?

Yes — short-term rental operators in Key West are generally required to obtain a state of Florida vacation rental license as well as comply with Monroe County and City of Key West regulations, which can include zoning restrictions and transient rental tax registration.
