Why Savannah, Georgia Is a Top Short-Term Rental Market

Savannah's appeal as a short-term rental destination stems from a rare combination of history, hospitality, and high visitation. The city attracts roughly 14 million visitors annually, fueled by the landmark St. Patrick's Day festival, SCAD arts events, film tourism, and a thriving food-and-drink scene. That sustained demand creates a favorable environment for STR investors across multiple price points.

Popular neighborhoods for short-term rentals include the Victorian District, Thomas Square, Starland, and the Landmark Historic District — areas where walkability and character command premium nightly rates. Markets like Savannah often see occupancy rates well above the national average during peak periods, with many operators reporting strong performance from late spring through the holiday season.

  • Year-round leisure and group travel demand
  • Historic architecture that photographs well and attracts repeat guests
  • Proximity to Tybee Island extending the rental draw beyond the city core
  • A growing corporate and relocation travel segment

What permits or regulations do I need to short-term rent a property in Savannah, GA?

Savannah requires short-term rental operators to obtain a city business license and, in most cases, a Short-Term Vacation Rental permit, with rules that vary depending on whether the property is owner-occupied. Certain historic overlay zones also carry additional restrictions, so it is important to review current City of Savannah ordinances before listing. E&J Retreats recommends consulting a local attorney or the city's Revenue Department to confirm your property's compliance status before launching.
