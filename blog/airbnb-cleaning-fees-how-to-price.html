Airbnb Cleaning Fees: How to Price Them Without Losing Bookings

June 15, 2026 6 min read STR Strategy

With the right strategy, your cleaning fee can actually work for your listing rather than against it.

Why Airbnb Cleaning Fees Kill Bookings (And How to Avoid It)

The core problem with cleaning fees is a psychological one. When a guest searches Airbnb, they see a nightly rate — but when they go to book, the total price jumps significantly after fees are added. This gap between the advertised rate and the checkout total is called "price shock," and it's one of the leading reasons guests abandon bookings.

Airbnb's algorithm has also shifted over the years to favor total price transparency. Listings with disproportionately high cleaning fees relative to their nightly rate tend to rank lower in search results and see lower conversion rates. If your $90/night cabin has a $150 cleaning fee, a three-night stay suddenly costs guests $50+ per night just in fees — and that math is very visible to them.

The Cleaning Fee-to-Nightly Rate Ratio That Actually Works

A practical benchmark most experienced hosts follow is keeping your cleaning fee at or below 30–40% of your average nightly rate. For a property averaging $150 per night, that means a cleaning fee somewhere in the $45–$60 range tends to maintain strong search performance and booking conversion. Of course, larger homes with higher turnover costs will naturally carry higher fees — the key is that the ratio stays reasonable.

Pro Tip: Use Minimum Night Stays Strategically

One of the most effective ways to offset a higher cleaning fee is to set a 2-night minimum stay. This spreads the fee across more nights in the guest's calculation, making the per-night cost feel far more reasonable — and it protects you from costly one-night turnovers.

How to Actually Set Your Airbnb Cleaning Fees the Right Way

Setting the right cleaning fee isn't guesswork — it requires knowing your real turnover costs and understanding what competing properties in your market are charging. Here's a structured approach to pricing your cleaning fee without sacrificing bookings:

  • Calculate your true cleaning cost: Add up labor (cleaner's hourly rate × time), supplies, laundry, and any inspection fees. This is your floor — never charge less than this.
  • Survey your competitive set: Look at 5–10 comparable listings in your market with similar bed/bath counts and check their cleaning fees. You want to be within range, not an outlier.
  • Factor in stay length: Some hosts use dynamic cleaning fees or adjust their minimum stay based on season. A longer average stay means the cleaning fee is less of a per-night burden on guests.
  • Check your search ranking after changes: After adjusting your cleaning fee, monitor your listing's impressions and click-through rate. A drop in views can signal the algorithm is deprioritizing you.
  • Consider absorbing some cost into the nightly rate: Spreading part of the cleaning cost into a slightly higher nightly rate can reduce the shock effect while keeping your revenue neutral.

The "Total Price" Filter Is Changing the Game

Airbnb now defaults to showing guests a total price view — which means cleaning fees are more visible than ever before. Listings that previously hid behind a low nightly rate and a high cleaning fee are being penalized in search. This makes it more important than ever to find a genuine balance rather than using the cleaning fee as a hidden revenue tool. Ongoing Cleaning Fee Optimization: It's Not Set-and-Forget

Market conditions, competition, and even seasonal demand shifts can all affect what cleaning fee the market will bear. A fee that worked perfectly in 2024 may be hurting you in 2026 as more inventory enters your market and guests become increasingly price-sensitive. Revisiting your Airbnb cleaning fees quarterly — and comparing them against current comps — is a habit that separates consistently high-performing hosts from those who plateau.
