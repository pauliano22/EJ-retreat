The Breckenridge Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Breckenridge consistently ranks among the top ski towns in the country for short-term rental demand. The town's combination of a renowned ski resort, a vibrant Main Street, summer hiking and biking trails, and a packed events calendar means that demand rarely disappears — it simply shifts between seasons. Many STR operators in markets like Breckenridge report occupancy rates well above national averages, particularly during peak winter and summer windows.

Popular neighborhoods for STR investment include areas near the base of Peak 8 and Peak 9, properties within walking distance of Main Street, and mountain-view cabins just outside town. Investors are drawn to Breckenridge for several reasons:

  • Year-round demand from ski season, summer recreation, and fall foliage visitors
  • A strong luxury traveler segment willing to pay premium nightly rates
  • Proximity to Denver International Airport, making it accessible for weekend getaways
  • A well-established vacation rental culture with traveler familiarity

Average daily rates in Breckenridge typically outperform many comparable mountain markets, and well-located properties frequently command top-tier pricing during holiday weeks and powder days.

Revenue Potential for Short-Term Rentals in Breckenridge

Revenue potential in Breckenridge is driven by a powerful combination of seasonal peaks and sustained shoulder-season demand. Winter ski season — particularly holiday weeks around Christmas, New Year's, and Presidents' Day — typically produces the highest nightly rates of the year, with well-managed properties often seeing their strongest occupancy and ADR simultaneously. Summer is increasingly strong as well, with mountain bikers, hikers, and attendees of Breckenridge's music and arts festivals filling calendars from June through August.

Many operators in markets like Breckenridge report annual gross revenues ranging from the mid-five figures for smaller condos to well into six figures for larger, well-positioned homes.
