Why Chattanooga Is a Strong Market for Short-Term Rental Investors

Chattanooga's tourism economy is fueled by a diverse mix of demand drivers that keep short-term rental calendars full throughout the year. The city's outdoor recreation scene — anchored by Lookout Mountain, the Tennessee River Gorge, and miles of biking and hiking trails — draws visitors in every season, while cultural events, sporting tournaments, and a growing corporate corridor attract both leisure and business guests.

Investors looking at Chattanooga's STR landscape will find a range of high-performing pockets, including:

  • North Shore and Coolidge Park, popular for walkability and riverfront access
  • Downtown Chattanooga, close to the Tennessee Aquarium and entertainment districts
  • St. Elmo and Lookout Mountain neighborhoods, favored for charm and proximity to outdoor attractions
  • East Brainerd and suburban corridors attracting extended-stay and family travelers

Markets like Chattanooga often see strong average daily rates during peak summer months and fall foliage season, with many operators reporting solid occupancy well above regional averages when listings are professionally optimized and well-reviewed.
