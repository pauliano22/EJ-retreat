STR Turnover Cleaning Challenges and Standards in New Orleans

New Orleans hosts a relentless calendar of events — Mardi Gras, Jazz Fest, Essence Fest, and countless smaller gatherings — that compress bookings and push same-day turnovers to the limit. Many operators report back-to-back checkouts where a cleaner may have as little as two to three hours to fully reset a property before the next guest arrives. That pace demands systems, not improvisation.

STR turnover cleaning differs fundamentally from routine residential cleaning. Every visit must cover a full reset: laundering all linens and towels, restocking consumables, inspecting for damage, sanitizing high-touch surfaces, and staging the property to look guest-ready in photos. Markets like New Orleans often see elevated wear on properties near the French Quarter or the Garden District, where party-heavy bookings can leave a unit in need of deep attention even between standard cleans.

  • Poor linen turnover creates hygiene complaints that damage long-term reputation
