Why Charleston, SC Is One of the Southeast's Strongest STR Markets

Charleston draws a diverse mix of travelers year-round — romantic getaway couples, bachelorette parties, history enthusiasts, and corporate visitors attending the Port of Charleston's busy conference calendar. That diversity helps insulate the market from seasonal swings that hit more one-dimensional destinations hard.

Popular short-term rental neighborhoods include the French Quarter and Cannonborough-Elliotborough on the Charleston Peninsula, as well as James Island, West Ashley, and the beach communities of Sullivan's Island and Folly Beach. Each micro-market carries its own demand profile and guest expectations.

  • Strong leisure demand tied to historic sites, restaurants, and outdoor recreation
  • Year-round events including SPOLETO Festival, Charleston Wine + Food, and Riverfront concerts
  • Many operators report average daily rates (ADR) well above national STR medians for comparable property types
  • Proximity to beaches, the Harbor, and I-26 makes diverse property types viable investments

Regulations have evolved in recent years as the city balances housing concerns with tourism demand, so staying current is important.
