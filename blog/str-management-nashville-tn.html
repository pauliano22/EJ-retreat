Nashville's Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Investors

Nashville consistently ranks among the top short-term rental markets in the Southeast. The city draws tens of millions of visitors annually, fueled by Broadway honky-tonks, the Grand Ole Opry, stadium events, and a convention calendar that fills hotels and STRs alike. Neighborhoods like East Nashville, The Gulch, Germantown, and 12South are especially popular with Airbnb guests seeking walkable, character-rich stays.

Well-positioned Nashville STR properties typically see strong average daily rates, with many operators reporting ADRs well above the national median, particularly during major events and peak summer weekends. Occupancy rates in desirable Nashville zip codes can reach 70–85% for professionally managed listings.

Do I need a permit or license to operate a short-term rental in Nashville?

Nashville and Davidson County require short-term rental property owners to obtain an operating permit, and the city distinguishes between owner-occupied and non-owner-occupied STR permits. Regulations can change, so it is important to verify current requirements with Metro Nashville's codes department before listing your property.
