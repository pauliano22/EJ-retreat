The Joshua Tree Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Joshua Tree occupies a rare position in the STR landscape: a drive-market destination within easy reach of Los Angeles and San Diego that draws a broad mix of travelers, from weekend hikers and stargazers to digital nomads and wedding groups. That diversity of demand gives well-positioned properties a longer booking window than many comparable desert markets.

Popular areas for investment include the Yucca Valley corridor, the park-adjacent streets near the town of Joshua Tree, and quieter pockets like Pioneertown, each attracting different guest profiles. Many operators report strong occupancy across both peak winter weekends and summer shoulder periods, with average daily rates in desirable properties typically ranging well above state and national STR averages.

  • High demand driven by Joshua Tree National Park visitation (millions of annual visitors)
  • Strong repeat-guest and word-of-mouth bookings for distinctive desert properties
  • Growing appetite for unique stays: dome homes, A-frames, and architect-designed retreats command premium ADR
  • Drive-market resilience — demand holds even when air travel softens

Do I need a permit or license to operate a short-term rental in Joshua Tree?

Yes — San Bernardino County, which governs most of the Joshua Tree area, requires short-term rental operators to obtain a permit and comply with local regulations covering occupancy limits, noise, and parking. Requirements can vary if your property falls within a city jurisdiction rather than the unincorporated county.
