The Palm Springs Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Palm Springs attracts millions of visitors annually, fueled by festivals like Coachella and Stagecoach, the Modernism Week showcase, golf tournaments, and a thriving spa and wellness tourism sector. That concentrated demand creates strong occupancy windows and elevated average daily rates that make the area a compelling target for STR investors.

Popular neighborhoods for short-term rentals include the Movie Colony, Vista Las Palmas, the Tennis Club District, and South Palm Springs — each offering a distinct aesthetic that appeals to different traveler segments. Many operators report average daily rates ranging from $250 to $600 or more for well-appointed properties, with peak season running from October through May.

  • World-famous festival weekends that can command 3–5x typical nightly rates
  • High repeat-visitor rates driven by the area's lifestyle appeal
  • Strong demand from drive-market travelers in Southern California and Arizona
  • A diverse inventory of pool homes, villas, and casitas that attract premium guests

Does Palm Springs allow short-term rentals, and do I need a permit?

Palm Springs does permit short-term rentals, but hosts are required to obtain a vacation rental permit from the City of Palm Springs and comply with local rules including occupancy limits, noise ordinances, and transient occupancy tax (TOT) collection. Regulations have evolved in recent years, so it is important to verify current requirements with the city before listing.
