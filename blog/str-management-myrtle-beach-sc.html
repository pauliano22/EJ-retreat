The Myrtle Beach Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Owners

Myrtle Beach consistently ranks among the most-visited beach destinations on the East Coast, and that visitor volume translates directly into short-term rental demand. The market benefits from a diverse traveler mix — families during summer, golf enthusiasts in spring and fall, and snowbirds through the winter months — which helps smooth out seasonal dips that other coastal markets experience.

Popular neighborhoods and corridors for STR investors include the oceanfront Grand Strand, North Myrtle Beach, Surfside Beach, and the resort-style communities along Kings Highway. Many operators in markets like Myrtle Beach report average daily rates (ADR) that climb well above $150 during peak summer weeks, with strong occupancy rates from May through September.

  • Year-round demand from beach, golf, and convention travelers
  • High density of vacation-ready condo and cottage inventory
  • Proximity to Myrtle Beach International Airport driving short-notice bookings
  • A permissive local regulatory environment compared to many coastal cities

Revenue Potential for Well-Managed Short-Term Rentals in Myrtle Beach

The revenue a Myrtle Beach short-term rental earns depends on property type, location, and how well it is managed. Beachfront condos and homes with ocean views typically command the highest ADR, while properties a few blocks inland can still achieve strong occupancy by competing on value. Many operators in comparable coastal markets report annual gross revenues ranging from $30,000 to over $80,000 depending on unit size and amenities.

Seasonal demand in Myrtle Beach follows a predictable pattern: peak rates from Memorial Day through Labor Day, a secondary spike around spring break and major golf events, and softer but still bookable winter months. Professional management closes the gap between self-managing owners — who often leave shoulder-season nights vacant — and optimized listings that stay competitive year-round.

Owners who switch to professional STR management in markets like Myrtle Beach typically see improved occupancy, fewer gaps between stays, and higher average nightly rates, all without the time investment of handling operations themselves.
