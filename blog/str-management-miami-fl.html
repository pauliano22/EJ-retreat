Miami's Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Owners

Miami consistently ranks among the top STR markets in the country, fueled by a combination of leisure travel, corporate relocations, and major events like Art Basel, Ultra Music Festival, and the Formula 1 Grand Prix. Demand is not limited to a single season — while winter draws snowbirds and spring break brings college travelers, summer sees strong domestic and Latin American tourism that keeps occupancy healthy throughout the year.

Popular neighborhoods for short-term rentals in Miami include South Beach, Wynwood, Brickell, Coconut Grove, and Little Havana, each attracting a distinct traveler profile. Markets like Miami often see average daily rates well above national benchmarks, and many operators report strong occupancy figures in the 70–85% range for well-positioned properties.

  • Year-round international and domestic tourism demand
  • High average daily rates driven by limited hotel supply in residential areas
  • Diverse guest profiles: leisure, business, relocation, and event travelers
  • Strong repeat-visitor rates in beachside and cultural neighborhoods
