Ocean City Maryland's Short-Term Rental Market: Why It's a Strong Opportunity for Investors

Ocean City is one of the most visited beach destinations on the East Coast, attracting families, couples, and groups from major feeder markets including Baltimore, Washington D.C., Philadelphia, and Richmond. That concentrated regional demand — paired with a limited housing supply directly on or near the water — creates favorable conditions for short-term rental investors.

Popular areas for STR properties include the Oceanfront and Bayside corridors, North Ocean City neighborhoods near 90th Street and above, and condo-heavy zones along the mid-island strip. Properties with ocean views, private pools, or proximity to the boardwalk typically command the strongest nightly rates.

  • Peak summer weeks (late June through August) often drive the majority of annual revenue
  • Shoulder seasons — Memorial Day weekend, fall festivals, and holiday weekends — add meaningful occupancy
  • Markets like Ocean City often see occupancy rates of 55–75% annually for well-managed listings
  • Average daily rates during peak summer periods can range widely depending on property size and amenities
