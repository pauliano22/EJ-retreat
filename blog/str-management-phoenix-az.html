Phoenix's Short-Term Rental Market: A Strong Opportunity for Property Investors

Phoenix consistently ranks among the top US cities for short-term rental investment, driven by a combination of warm weather, a growing tourism sector, and a steady stream of business travelers. The metro area hosts major recurring events — including MLB spring training, the Barrett-Jackson auto auction, and PGA Tour stops — that create reliable demand spikes throughout the year.

Popular neighborhoods for STR investors include Scottsdale (Old Town and the resort corridor), Tempe near Arizona State University, downtown Phoenix, and Arcadia, each attracting different guest profiles from snowbirds to event-goers. Many operators report strong performance in shoulder seasons as well, thanks to Phoenix's appeal as a hiking, golf, and wellness destination.

  • Year-round warm climate supports extended booking seasons
  • High-demand event calendar drives premium nightly rates
  • Diverse guest mix: leisure travelers, business visitors, and snowbirds
  • Growing population and tourism infrastructure support long-term market health

Revenue Potential for Well-Managed STR Properties in Phoenix

Revenue for Phoenix short-term rentals varies widely based on property size, location, and management quality, but markets like Phoenix often see strong average daily rates (ADR) for well-positioned listings. Professionally managed properties in high-demand areas such as Scottsdale and Tempe can typically achieve significantly higher ADR than the metro average, particularly during peak event weekends when rates may increase several times over baseline.

Seasonal patterns matter in Phoenix: winter months (November through March) attract snowbirds and warm-weather seekers, while spring training in February and March generates some of the highest demand of the year. Summer tends to be softer, but competitive pricing and targeted marketing can maintain healthy occupancy even during hotter months.

Many self-managing landlords leave revenue on the table by not adjusting rates frequently enough or by missing key distribution channels. What are Phoenix's rules and regulations for short-term rentals?

Arizona state law generally preempts local municipalities from outright banning short-term rentals, which makes Phoenix and the surrounding metro relatively STR-friendly compared to many other major US cities. However, property owners are typically required to register with the city, collect and remit applicable taxes, and comply with health and safety standards.
