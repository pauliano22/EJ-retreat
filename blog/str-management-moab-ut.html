The Moab Short-Term Rental Market Opportunity

Moab sits at the gateway to two national parks and a network of iconic outdoor recreation destinations, making it one of Utah's most resilient short-term rental markets. Demand is driven by adventure travelers, family road-trippers, mountain bikers, and off-road enthusiasts who need flexible, home-like accommodations rather than limited hotel rooms.

Popular areas for STR investment in Moab include downtown properties within walking distance of restaurants and outfitters, as well as canyon-view retreats on the outskirts of town. Many operators report strong occupancy across multiple seasons, with peak demand concentrated around spring and fall when temperatures are ideal for outdoor activities.

  • Proximity to Arches and Canyonlands National Parks drives consistent bookings
  • Spring and fall shoulder seasons typically produce some of the highest ADRs
  • Adventure and outdoor recreation guests tend to book longer stays
  • Limited hotel inventory in the area supports strong STR demand

Revenue Potential for Short-Term Rentals in Moab

Markets like Moab often see above-average nightly rates compared to other Utah destinations, thanks to the strong draw of national park tourism and limited lodging supply. Well-positioned properties — those with outdoor spaces, canyon views, or high-quality amenities — typically command premium ADRs, particularly during spring, fall, and holiday weekends.

Many Moab STR owners report that professional management meaningfully increases their annual revenue compared to self-managing, largely through smarter pricing, higher review scores from consistent guest experiences, and broader distribution across booking channels. Seasonal demand in Moab tends to smooth out over the year more than many mountain markets, with winter shoulder seasons still supported by mild weather and special events.

  • Spring and fall typically deliver peak occupancy and highest nightly rates
  • Summer heat creates a softer mid-season, offset by consistent shoulder demand
  • Holiday weekends and event weekends regularly outperform baseline rates
  • Professional management can reduce vacancy and increase revenue per available night
