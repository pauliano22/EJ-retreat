Pricing Strategy

How We Think About Rates

Flat-rate pricing leaves thousands on the table every year. Here's how we do it differently.

  • Seasonality Curves

    We map out your market's peak, shoulder, and off-season periods and price accordingly — capturing premium rates when demand is high and staying competitive when it's slow.

  • Event-Based Spikes

    Concerts, sports events, conferences, and local festivals drive massive demand surges. We monitor local event calendars and raise rates proactively — often 2–3x on high-demand weekends.

  • Days-Until-Arrival Pricing

    A booking 90 days out is priced differently than one 3 days out. We use time-to-arrival curves to maximize revenue early and fill gaps last-minute without steep discounting.

  • Length-of-Stay Optimization

    Weekend minimums, weekday pricing adjustments, and gap-filling strategies — we configure minimum stays dynamically so your calendar fills efficiently without leaving open nights.

  • Competitor Benchmarking

    We continuously monitor what comparable listings in your area are charging and how they're performing. Your rates stay positioned to win bookings without underpricing your property.

  • Property Size Segmentation

    Pricing a 2BR is completely different from pricing a 5BR pool home. We build custom rate profiles based on your exact bedroom count, amenity set, and the demand curve for that property type in your market.

Why Flat Rates Don't Work

Most self-managing owners set a rate and leave it. The market moves every single day — demand shifts, competitors adjust, events come and go. A static rate means you're overpriced when demand is low (empty nights) and underpriced when demand is high (money left on the table).

Dynamic pricing isn't about discounting — it's about capturing the right price at the right moment. Properties we manage typically see their revenue increase within the first 60–90 days simply from pricing corrections alone.

Combined with better listing visibility, more channels, and professional guest communication, the revenue improvement often covers our management fee entirely — and then some.

FAQ

Common Questions About STR Management

Everything owners ask before getting started.

What is short-term rental management?

STR management means a company handles all aspects of renting your property on platforms like Airbnb and VRBO — pricing, listings, guest communication, channel distribution, and performance optimization — in exchange for a percentage of your rental revenue.

How does a channel manager work?

A channel manager connects your property to multiple booking platforms (Airbnb, VRBO, Booking.com, etc.) through a single dashboard. When a booking comes in on any platform, all other calendars update instantly — eliminating double bookings and manual calendar management.

What is dynamic pricing and why does it matter?

Dynamic pricing means your nightly rate adjusts automatically based on demand signals — local events, seasonality, competitor rates, days until arrival, and booking pace. Static pricing almost always means overpriced slow nights and underpriced peak nights, costing owners thousands per year.

How is your fee calculated exactly?

We take a percentage of your gross rental revenue after the cleaning fee is subtracted. For example, if a guest pays $1,000 and the cleaning fee is $150, our fee is calculated on $850. Core Management is 10%, Full Service is 15%.

What's the difference between Airbnb and direct booking?

Airbnb charges guests a 12–15% service fee on top of your nightly rate. Direct bookings bypass that fee entirely — guests pay less, you earn more, and there's no OTA taking a cut. We build you a direct booking website as part of onboarding.

How long does onboarding take?

Most properties are fully live within 7–14 days of signing. This includes the onboarding form, strategy call, listing build-out, channel manager activation, and direct booking website setup. We move fast because every day offline is revenue lost.

What is ADR and why does it matter?

ADR stands for Average Daily Rate — the average amount you earn per booked night. It's one of the two key performance metrics for STR properties (the other being occupancy rate). A higher ADR means more revenue per booking, which is why pricing strategy matters so much.

Can I block dates for personal use?

Absolutely. You can block any dates you want at any time. Your property is still yours — we just manage it when it's available for guests. We ask for reasonable notice when possible so we can adjust pricing around your blocks.

What markets do you serve?

We're based in Tampa, FL and primarily serve the Tampa Bay area and surrounding Florida markets. We also manage properties in other STR-strong markets — reach out to discuss your specific location.

Do I need professional photos?

Professional photos are the single highest-ROI investment you can make in your STR listing. We strongly recommend them and can advise on what to shoot. Great photos directly increase click-through rates, booking conversion, and the rates guests are willing to pay.

What happens if a guest damages something?

Airbnb's AirCover program provides up to $3M in host damage protection. VRBO has similar coverage. We document your property thoroughly during onboarding so any damage claims are supported with detailed pre-arrival records.

How do I know how my property is performing?

We send monthly performance reports covering your revenue, occupancy rate, ADR, and a market comparison showing how you stack up against similar properties nearby. You'll always have full visibility into how your investment is performing.
